Fuel Cost Calculator: Plan Your Trip Budget

With rising petrol and diesel prices, road trips require financial planning. Whether you are driving daily to work or planning a cross-country expedition, knowing your fuel cost is essential. This Fuel Cost Calculator does the math for you, considering your vehicle's mileage, the total distance, and current fuel prices.

Variables that Affect Fuel Cost

1. Mileage (Fuel Efficiency)

Measured in km/l (Kilometers per Liter) or MPG (Miles Per Gallon).
A car giving 15 km/l is 50% cheaper to run than one giving 10 km/l.

2. Driving Style

Aggressive acceleration and braking can lower your mileage by 15-30%. Cruising at a constant speed is most efficient.

3. Load and AC

A fully loaded car with roof luggage and AC running will consume significantly more fuel.

Formulas

Fuel Required (L): Distance / Mileage.
Total Cost (₹): Fuel Required × Price per Liter.

Tips to Save Fuel

  • Check Tyre Pressure: Under-inflated tyres increase rolling resistance.
  • Reduce Weight: Remove unnecessary items from the boot.
  • Engine Idling: Turn off ignition at long traffic signals (>30s).
  • Service: A clean air filter and fresh oil improve efficiency.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. How to measure mileage accurately?

Tank-to-tank method. Fill full, drive X km, fill full again. Mileage = X / Liters filled.

2. Highway vs City?

Highway mileage is usually 20-30% better due to less braking.

3. Does AC waste fuel?

Yes, AC compressor uses engine power. Increases consumption by ~10%.

4. Windows open vs AC?

At high speeds (>80kmph), windows open creates drag, which is worse than using AC.

5. Premium petrol?

Higher octane. Only useful for high-performance engines. No benefit for regular city cars.

6. Cost per km?

Total Cost / Total Distance. Useful for splitting bills.

7. Electric vs Fuel?

EV running cost is ₹1/km vs Petrol ₹6-8/km.

8. Cold start?

Engines are inefficient when cold. Short trips use more fuel.

9. Cruise control?

Helps improve mileage on highways.

10. Weight effect?

Every 50kg extra weight reduces mileage by ~2%.
