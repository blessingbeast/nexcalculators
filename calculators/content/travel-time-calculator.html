What is a Travel Time Calculator?

A travel time calculator uses the fundamental Speed-Distance-Time relationship to estimate how long a journey will take. Given a known distance and expected average travel speed, it calculates the total time required. It's used for road trips, flight planning, shipping logistics, and daily commute estimation.

Formula

Time = Distance / Speed

Result in hours. Convert: Hours × 60 = Minutes. Hours = integer hours + decimal minutes.

Example Calculation

  • Delhi to Jaipur: 280 km at 70 km/h average → Time = 280/70 = 4 hours
  • Mumbai to Pune: 150 km at 80 km/h average → Time = 150/80 = 1 hour 52.5 minutes

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter the Distance in kilometers.
  2. Enter your Average Speed in km/h (be realistic — factor in traffic, tolls, and rest stops).
  3. Click Calculate to see the precise journey time in hours and minutes.

FAQs

What average speed should I use for Indian national highways?

On well-maintained expressways (like Mumbai-Pune), 80-90 km/h is realistic. On regular national highways with truck traffic and towns, 60-70 km/h is a safer estimate.

Should I add breaks to the travel time?

Yes. Add 30 minutes for every 3-4 hours of driving for fuel, food, and rest. Long trips (6+ hours) require at least 1 hour of break time for safety.
