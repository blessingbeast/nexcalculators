What is Sales Tax?

Sales tax is a consumption tax imposed by the government on the sale of goods and services. It is collected by the retailer at the point of purchase and then passed on to the government. The rate varies significantly by country, state, and even city, making a quick calculation tool essential for accurate budgeting.

Formula

Sales Tax = Base Amount × (Tax Rate / 100)

Total Amount = Base Amount + Sales Tax

Example

Scenario: You are buying a smartphone for ₹20,000 in a region with 12% sales tax.

  • Base Amount = ₹20,000
  • Tax Rate = 12%
  • Sales Tax = ₹20,000 × 0.12 = ₹2,400
  • Total Price = ₹20,000 + ₹2,400 = ₹22,400

How to Use

  1. Enter Base Amount: The price of the product before tax.
  2. Enter Tax Rate: The percentage of sales tax (e.g., 5, 12, 18).
  3. Click Calculate: Instantly see the tax component and your final bill.
