What is Probability?

Probability quantifies how likely an event is to occur. It ranges from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain). Expressed as a percentage, 0 = 0% chance and 1 = 100% chance. Probability theory underpins statistics, finance, insurance, machine learning, game design, and everyday risk assessment.

Formula

P(Event) = Favorable Outcomes / Total Possible Outcomes

Percentage Probability = P(Event) × 100

Odds = Favorable : Unfavorable = F : (T−F)

Example Calculation

  • Rolling a 4 on a die: P = 1/6 = 16.67%
  • Drawing an Ace from a 52-card deck: P = 4/52 = 1/13 = 7.69%
  • Tossing Heads on a coin: P = 1/2 = 50%

FAQs

Can probability be greater than 1?

No. Probability always falls between 0 and 1 (or 0% to 100%). If your calculation yields more than 1, your denominator (total outcomes) must be incorrect.

What is the complement of a probability?

P(Not A) = 1 − P(A). If the probability of rain is 30%, the probability of no rain is 70%.
