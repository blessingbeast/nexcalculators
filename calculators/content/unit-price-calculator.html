What is Unit Price?

Unit price is the cost of a single unit of a product, such as a liter, kilogram, or ounce. It is the most reliable way to compare the costs of different brands or package sizes. Grocery stores often hide high prices behind "convenience" sizes, and this tool helps you avoid that trap by identifying the most cost-effective option.

Formula

Unit Price = Total Price / Quantity

Ensure both products use the same units for a fair comparison.

Example

Scenario: Brand A detergent costs ₹500 for 2.5 Liters. Brand B costs ₹650 for 3.2 Liters.

  • Brand A: ₹500 / 2.5 = ₹200 per Liter
  • Brand B: ₹650 / 3.2 = ₹203.12 per Liter
  • Result: Brand A is the better deal despite having less volume.

How to Use

  1. Total Price: What you are paying for the package.
  2. Quantity: The number of units in the package (weight, volume, or count).
  3. Click Calculate: Get the individual unit cost instantly.
