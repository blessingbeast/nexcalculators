What Is Unit Price?

Unit price is the cost of a single standard measure of a product — for example, the price per gram, per liter, or per piece. It is the most reliable and fair way to compare products of different sizes, brands, or package configurations. Rather than guessing whether a 500g box at ₹100 is better than a 750g box at ₹140, the unit price calculator best deal tells you immediately and definitively which one is cheaper per gram.

In India, where packaging sizes and weights vary enormously between brands and between local neighbourhood stores and supermarkets, knowing the unit price is your strongest defense against paying over the odds.

The Unit Price Formula

The math behind unit pricing is a simple division. Our tool applies it in two ways — to compare two products directly, or to find your own unit cost:

Unit Price = Total Price / Quantity

Winner = The product with the lower Unit Price

The Supermarket "Perceived Value" Trap

Retailers use a well-known trick called "value anchoring" — placing the large "economy" pack next to the individual pack to make the large one seem like a massive saving. However, in many cases, the individual pack at a convenience store has a lower unit price than a supposedly "bulk deal" at a hypermarket. FAQs

1. What units should I use for the comparison to work?

Both products must be measured in the same unit for a fair comparison. If Pack A is in grams and Pack B is in kilograms, convert them first (1 kg = 1000 g).

2. Does a lower unit price always mean better value?

Mathematically yes, but practically, consider factors like shelf life, your storage capacity, and whether you will actually use all of a bulk-bought product before it expires or goes stale.

3. Can I use this for services (like data plans)?

Yes! You can compare mobile data plans by their "price per GB" or subscription plans by their "price per month." Any price-to-quantity comparison works.

4. Are online prices better than retail prices in India?

Not always. Factor in delivery charges. A ₹50 delivery fee can significantly increase the unit price of low-cost items. Use our tool to find the true cost including shipping.
