What is Markup?

Markup is the amount added to the cost price of a product or service to cover operational overhead and generate a profit. Usually expressed as a percentage of the original cost, markup ensures that every sale contributes to the financial health of the business. While people often confuse markup with "margin," markup specifically looks at how much you "mark up" your cost to reach a sale price.

Using a markup calculator profit percentage tool is vital for retailers and wholesalers who need to manage "keystone pricing" or dynamic discounts while maintaining a strict bottom line.

The Markup Formula

Calculating markup is a straightforward two-step process. Our online tool automates the math to prevent costly pricing errors:

Markup Amount = Cost × (Markup % / 100)

Final Selling Price = Cost + Markup Amount

Step-by-Step Example: Pricing Handcrafted Goods

To understand the markup calculator profit percentage in practice, imagine an artisan who creates custom leather wallets.

  • Step 1: Determine your cost. Materials and labor cost ₹1,200 per wallet.
  • Step 2: Choose your markup. The artisan wants a 60% markup to cover marketing and profit.
  • Step 3: Calculate the markup amount. ₹1,200 × 0.60 = ₹720.
  • Step 4: Find the selling price. ₹1,200 + ₹720 = ₹1,920.

By pricing the wallet at ₹1,920, the artisan covers their costs and retains ₹720 for business growth.

Markup vs. Gross Margin: The Big Difference

A frequent mistake for new business owners is treating markup and margin as the same thing. Markup is profit as a percentage of cost. Margin is profit as a percentage of selling price.

If you want a 50% margin, you actually need a 100% markup (doubling your cost). If you only apply a 50% markup, your gross margin will be only 33.3%. Our markup calculator profit percentage tool shows you both numbers so you never underprice your goods.

FAQs

1. What is a "good" markup percentage?

Markups vary wildly by industry. Traditional retail often uses a 100% markup (doubling the price), while high-volume grocery items might have an 8-15% markup.

2. Does markup include GST?

Typically, markup is calculated on the pre-tax cost. GST is then added to the final selling price. Check our GST calculator to see how taxes impact your final customer bill.

3. How do I calculate markup if I only know the selling price?

Markup % = ((Selling Price - Cost) / Cost) × 100. Our tool can help you back-calculate these values easily.
