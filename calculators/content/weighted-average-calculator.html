What is a Weighted Average?

A weighted average, also known as a weighted mean, is a calculation that takes into account the varying degrees of importance of the numbers in a dataset. Unlike a simple average where every number contributes equally, a weighted average multiplies each value by its weight, sums them up, and then divides by the total of all weights. This is widely used in academic grading (where a final exam counts more than a quiz) and investment analysis.

Formula

Weighted Average = Σ(Value × Weight) / ΣWeights

Σ denotes the sum of the elements.

Example

Scenario: You got a 90 on a quiz (worth 40% weight) and an 80 on a final (worth 60% weight).

  • (90 × 0.40) + (80 × 0.60) = 36 + 48 = 84
  • Sum of weights = 0.40 + 0.60 = 1
  • Weighted Average = 84 / 1 = 84%
