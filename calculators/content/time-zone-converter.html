What is Time Zone Conversion?

Time zone conversion is the process of adjusting a local clock time to represent the same "moment" in another geographical region. Since the Earth rotates, regions are divided into 24 standard zones relative to the Prime Meridian (UTC/GMT). This tool handles the offsets so you don't have to.

Formula

Target Time = Local Time + (Target Offset - Local Offset)

Example: IST is UTC+5.5, EST is UTC-5. Difference is 10.5 hours.

Example

Scenario: It is 2:00 PM in India (IST, UTC+5:30) and you want to know the time in New York (EST, UTC-5:00).

  • Source Time = 14:00
  • Difference = -10.5 Hours
  • Target Time = 14:00 - 10:30 = 3:30 AM in New York.
