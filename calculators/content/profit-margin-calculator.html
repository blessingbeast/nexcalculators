What Is Profit Margin?

Profit margin is the percentage of revenue that remains after deducting the cost of goods sold (COGS) or operating expenses. It is one of the most important financial metrics for any business, product, or freelance service. A higher margin means more profit per sale.

Profit Margin Formula

Profit Margin = (Revenue − Cost) ÷ Revenue × 100

Gross Profit = Revenue − Cost

Markup % = (Revenue − Cost) ÷ Cost × 100

Example Calculation

Scenario: You sell a product for ₹10,000 and it costs ₹7,000 to produce.

  • Gross Profit = ₹10,000 − ₹7,000 = ₹3,000
  • Profit Margin = ₹3,000 ÷ ₹10,000 × 100 = 30%
  • Markup = ₹3,000 ÷ ₹7,000 × 100 = 42.86%

Margin vs Markup — Key Difference

Margin = Profit as a percentage of selling price (Revenue). Used in financial reporting.
Markup = Profit as a percentage of cost. Used in pricing decisions.
If your markup is 43%, your margin is 30%. They are not the same!

What Is a Good Profit Margin?

  • Retail: 2–10%
  • Food & Beverage: 3–9%
  • Software/SaaS: 60–80%
  • Professional Services: 25–35%
  • E-commerce: 10–20%

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is a 30% profit margin?

It means 30 paise of every rupee in revenue is profit. For ₹1 lakh in revenue, ₹30,000 is profit.

2. Is higher margin always better?

Generally yes, but very high margins can indicate underpricing risk. Balance margin with competitive pricing.

3. How do I price a product for 40% margin?

Price = Cost ÷ (1 − Margin%). For 40% margin on ₹600 cost: Price = ₹600 ÷ 0.60 = ₹1,000.

4. Net profit vs gross profit margin?

Gross margin subtracts only COGS. Net margin also subtracts operating expenses, taxes, and interest.
