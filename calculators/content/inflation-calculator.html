Inflation Calculator: The Silent Thief

You've heard your grandparents say, "In my time, a movie ticket cost ₹2!". Today it costs ₹300. That is Inflation. It is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is rising, and consequently, the purchasing power of currency is falling. This calculator helps you travel through time to see what money was worth then, and what it will be worth in the future.

Why Does Money Lose Value?

Economies grow, money supply increases, and demand rises. A small, steady inflation (2-4%) is actually consider good for the economy as it encourages spending.
However, for a saver, it is an enemy. If you hide ₹1 Lakh under your mattress today, in 10 years it will still be ₹1 Lakh, but it will buy only half the things it buys today.

The Rule of 72 (Inflation Edition)

Divide 72 by the inflation rate to see when your prices double.
At 6% inflation, prices double every 12 years (72/6 = 12).
So if you need ₹50,000/month today, you will need ₹1,00,000/month in 12 years just to survive.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is CPI?

Consumer Price Index. Basket of common goods used to measure specific inflation.

2. Is gold inflation-proof?

Historically, gold has been a good hedge against inflation.

3. Hyperinflation?

When inflation hits 50%+ per month. (e.g., Zimbabwe, Venezuela). Money becomes worthless.

4. Deflation?

Prices going down. Rare and bad for economy (leads to recession).

5. Lifestyle Inflation?

Spending more as you earn more. This is personal, not economic.

6. Real Returns?

Return = Interest Rate - Inflation Rate.

7. Education Inflation?

Higher than general inflation. usually 10-12%.

8. Medical Inflation?

Highest in India, around 14%.

9. Who controls it?

Central Banks (RBI) use Repo Rates to control money supply and inflation.

10. Best hedge?

Equities (Stocks) and Real Estate generally beat inflation over long term.
