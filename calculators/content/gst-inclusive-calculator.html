What is a GST Inclusive Calculator?

A GST inclusive calculator works backwards from a tax-included price to find the original base price and the GST component separately. This is used by businesses for accurate bookkeeping (where input tax credit is claimed on the base price), by consumers verifying receipts, and by e-commerce sellers calculating their actual product revenue after GST is removed.

Formula

Base Price = GST-Inclusive Amount ÷ (1 + GST Rate/100)

GST Amount = GST-Inclusive Amount − Base Price

Example Calculation

You received ₹1,180 for a product sold with 18% GST included in the price.

  • Base Price = ₹1,180 / (1 + 18/100) = ₹1,180 / 1.18 = ₹1,000
  • GST Amount = ₹1,180 − ₹1,000 = ₹180
  • CGST (9%) = ₹90 | SGST (9%) = ₹90

FAQs

What GST slab does my product fall under?

Essential goods (milk, grains) = 0-5%. Processed foods, medicines = 5-12%. Electronics, clothing above ₹1000 = 18%. Luxury goods, tobacco = 28%. Check the GST tariff schedule for your specific product HSN code.

What is the difference between CGST and SGST?

For intra-state transactions, GST is split equally between CGST (Central GST) and SGST (State GST). For inter-state sales, only IGST (Integrated GST) applies at the full rate.

Is GST charged on the base price or final price?

GST is always applied to the base price (excluding GST). The total amount you pay equals: base price + GST amount.
