Income Tax Calculator: Decoding the New Regime

Income Tax is the price we pay for civilization. But calculating it can be a headache. With the introduction of the New Tax Regime (default from FY 2023-24), the calculations have changed significantly. This calculator focuses on the New Regime, providing a quick estimate of your liability.

Old vs New Regime

Old Regime: High tax rates, but allows many exemptions (HRA, 80C, 80D, Home Loan Interest). Good for people with heavy investments/loans.
New Regime: Lower tax rates, but NO exemptions (except Standard Deduction ₹50k/₹75k). Good for people who want cash in hand and simpler filing.

Slabs (New Regime FY 24-25)

  • 0 - 3L: Nil
  • 3 - 7L: 5% (Rebate available up to 7L, so Nil tax)
  • 7 - 10L: 10%
  • 10 - 12L: 15%
  • 12 - 15L: 20%
  • Above 15L: 30%

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Zero tax till 7 Lakhs?

Yes, due to Rebate u/s 87A. If income is ₹7,00,010, you pay tax on amounts above 3L.

2. Standard Deduction?

Increased to ₹75,000 for New Regime in latest budget proposals (check current FY).

3. Surcharge?

Extra tax on the rich. 10% if > 50L. 15% if > 1Cr. Capped at 25% for New Regime.

4. Cess?

4% Health & Education Cess is added to the Tax Payable.

5. Can I switch?

Salaried can switch every year. Business professionals can switch only once in lifetime.

6. PPF/LIC Benefits?

Only in Old Regime. In New Regime, they are just savings, no tax break.

7. Home Loan?

Interest deduction (₹2L) is only in Old Regime. (Unless it's a rented out property).

8. TDS vs Tax?

TDS is tax paid in advance. Final Tax is calculated during ITR. Refund happens if TDS > Final Tax.

9. ITR forms?

ITR-1 (Sahaj) for salary < 50L. ITR-2 for capital gains. ITR-3/4 for business.

10. Penalty?

Late filing attracts ₹5000 fine. Evasion attracts 50-200% penalty.
