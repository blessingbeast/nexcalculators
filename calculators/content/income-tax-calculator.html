Income tax calculation in India changed significantly with the introduction of the New Tax Regime, which became the default from FY 2023-24. This Income Tax Calculator helps you estimate your tax liability under the New Regime for FY 2025-26, so you can plan your salary, investments, and deductions smartly.

What Is Income Tax?

Income tax is a direct tax levied by the Government of India on the income earned by individuals, HUFs, and corporations. For salaried individuals, tax is deducted at source (TDS) by the employer every month and deposited with the government. The annual Income Tax Return (ITR) reconciles your total tax liability with what was already deducted.

Example Calculation

Scenario: Annual salary of ₹12,00,000. New Tax Regime.

  • Gross Taxable Income = ₹12,00,000 − ₹75,000 (Standard Deduction) = ₹11,25,000
  • Tax on ₹3–7L = ₹20,000 (5% of ₹4L)
  • Tax on ₹7–10L = ₹30,000 (10% of ₹3L)
  • Tax on ₹10–11.25L = ₹18,750 (15% of ₹1.25L)
  • Total Tax = ₹68,750
  • Add 4% Cess = ₹2,750
  • Total Tax Payable = ₹71,500

Old vs New Tax Regime — Which Is Better?

  • New Regime: Lower rates, simpler filing, no investment proof needed. Best for: income up to ₹10L with fewer deductions.
  • Old Regime: Higher rates but allows HRA, 80C (₹1.5L), 80D, home loan interest. Best for: people with high deductions exceeding ₹3-4L.
  • Break-Even Point: If your total deductions exceed ~₹3.75 lakh, old regime saves more tax.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is income up to ₹7 lakh really tax-free?

Yes, under the New Regime, Rebate u/s 87A makes effective tax Nil for income up to ₹7 lakhs (after standard deduction).

2. What is the standard deduction in New Regime?

₹75,000 for salaried employees and pensioners from FY 2024-25 onwards.

3. Can I switch between old and new tax regime every year?

Yes, salaried individuals can switch every year while filing ITR. Business income earners can switch only once.
