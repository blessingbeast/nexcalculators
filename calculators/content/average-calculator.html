What Is an Average (Mean)?

The arithmetic mean, commonly called the "average," is the sum of a set of numbers divided by the count of those numbers. It gives a central or typical value of a data set. For example, the average score in a class of 30 students tells you how well the class performed overall.

Average Formula

Average = Sum of all numbers ÷ Count of numbers

Also written as: x̄ = (x₁ + x₂ + ... + xₙ) ÷ n

Example Calculation

Scenario: Find the average of 45, 78, 92, 56, 88.

  • Sum = 45 + 78 + 92 + 56 + 88 = 359
  • Count = 5 numbers
  • Average = 359 ÷ 5 = 71.8

Mean vs Median vs Mode

  • Mean (Average): Sum ÷ Count. Best for normally distributed data without extreme outliers.
  • Median: Middle value when data is sorted. Better for skewed data (e.g., income distribution).
  • Mode: Most frequently occurring value. Useful for categorical data (e.g., most popular shoe size).

Real-World Examples

  • Grade calculation: Average of test scores to find final percentage
  • Weather: Average daily temperature to understand seasonal trends
  • Finance: Average stock price over a period for moving averages
  • Sports: Batting average, run rate, or goals per match

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is the difference between mean and average?

They are the same thing. "Mean" is the mathematical term; "average" is the common term for the arithmetic mean.

2. Can average be a decimal?

Yes. The average of 1, 2, 3 is 2, but the average of 1, 2 is 1.5. Decimals are perfectly valid averages.

3. Can I average negative numbers?

Yes. Average of -5, -3, 8 = (-5 + -3 + 8) ÷ 3 = 0 ÷ 3 = 0.

4. Why is average sometimes misleading?

Outliers heavily skew the mean. A group with one billionaire will have a high average income that doesn't represent most members.
