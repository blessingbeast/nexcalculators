What is a Salary Increment Calculator?

A salary increment calculator computes your revised salary after a percentage hike or a fixed amount increase. It shows both the new absolute salary and the incremental gain — useful for financial planning, salary negotiation, and understanding the real value of an offered increment percentage.

Formula

Increment Amount = Current Salary × (Hike % / 100)

New Salary = Current Salary + Increment Amount

Example Calculation

  • Current Monthly Salary: ₹55,000. Increment: 18%.
  • Increment Amount = ₹55,000 × 0.18 = ₹9,900/month more
  • New Monthly Salary = ₹64,900
  • New Annual CTC = ₹64,900 × 12 = ₹7,78,800

How to Use This Calculator

Real Life Use Cases

FAQs

What is the average salary hike in India?

Corporate India typically gives 8-12% annual hikes. Top performers receive 15-20%+. Employees changing jobs often see 20-40% hikes. The IT sector leads with the highest average increments.

Is the hike applied to gross salary or basic salary?

This varies by company. Most apply the hike to the entire CTC package. Some apply it only to the basic component, then recalculate allowances proportionally. Check your offer letter for clarity.
