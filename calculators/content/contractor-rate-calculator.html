Transitioning from employee to independent contractor? Our contractor rate calculator converts a permanent salary into an equivalent contractor day or hourly rate — accounting for the benefits, taxes, and business costs that contractors must cover independently.

What is a Contractor Rate Calculator?

A contractor rate calculator determines the minimum rate an independent contractor must charge to achieve earnings equivalent to a salaried employee. Contractors sacrifice employer PF, gratuity, paid leave, and job security — these hidden benefits typically add 25-40% to the true value of a permanent salary and must be built into the contractor rate.

Formula

Equivalent Revenue = Annual Salary × (1 + Overhead %/100)

Day Rate = Equivalent Revenue / Billable Days Per Year

Example Calculation

Salary equivalent: ₹12,00,000. Overhead (tax + benefits gap): 35%. Non-billable days: 30.

  • Revenue Needed = ₹12,00,000 × 1.35 = ₹16,20,000
  • Billable Days = 260 − 30 = 230 days
  • Day Rate = ₹16,20,000 / 230 = ₹7,043/day
  • Hourly Rate (8-hr day) = ₹880/hour

FAQs

Why do contractors typically earn more per hour?

Contractors bear risks employees don't: no guaranteed income, self-funded benefits, self-paid taxes, periods of no work, and business operating costs. Higher rates compensate for this additional risk and self-reliance.

What overhead percentage should I use?

A conservative estimate for Indian contractors: 12% employer PF equivalent + 4.8% gratuity provision + 10-15% income tax difference + 5-10% business expenses = approximately 30-40% total.
