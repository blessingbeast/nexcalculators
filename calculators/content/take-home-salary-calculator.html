What is Take Home Salary?

Take-home salary (also called "net salary" or "in-hand salary") is the amount credited to your bank account after all deductions from your gross monthly salary. In India, the primary deductions are Employee PF (12% of Basic salary), income tax TDS (based on your tax slab), professional tax (₹200/month in most states), and voluntary deductions like group insurance or loan EMI repayment.

Formula

Take Home = Gross Salary − (PF + TDS + Prof. Tax + Other Deductions)

Example Calculation

Gross Monthly Salary: ₹60,000. Employee PF: ₹7,200 (12%). TDS: ₹2,500. Prof. Tax: ₹200. Other: ₹1,000.

  • Total Deductions = ₹7,200 + ₹2,500 + ₹200 + ₹1,000 = ₹10,900
  • Take Home = ₹60,000 − ₹10,900 = ₹49,100/month

FAQs

What is the difference between CTC and take-home salary?

CTC (Cost to Company) includes employer PF (12%), gratuity provision, insurance, and other benefits the company pays on your behalf. Take-home is only what you personally receive after all deductions. Typically, take-home is 65-75% of CTC.

Can I reduce my TDS (income tax deduction)?

Yes. Submit Form 12BB to your employer declaring investments under Section 80C (up to ₹1.5L), HRA claims, home loan interest, and NPS contributions to reduce your monthly TDS.
