What Is a Retirement Calculator?

A retirement calculator helps you estimate the total saving corpus required to sustain your lifestyle after you stop working. By inputting your current age, planned retirement age, and current monthly expenses, this tool projects the lump sum you need saved by retirement day — giving you a concrete savings target.

Retirement Corpus Formula

Corpus Needed = Annual Expenses × 25

This is based on the popular 4% Safe Withdrawal Rule: if you withdraw 4% of your corpus annually, it should last 25+ years without depleting the principal (assuming ~7% investment returns).

Annual Expenses = Monthly Expenses × 12

Example Calculation

Scenario: You are 30 years old, plan to retire at 60, and your current monthly expenses are ₹50,000.

  • Annual Expenses = ₹50,000 × 12 = ₹6,00,000
  • Corpus Required = ₹6,00,000 × 25 = ₹1.5 Crore
  • Years to Retirement = 60 − 30 = 30 years
  • Monthly SIP needed at 12% return = ~₹8,600/month to reach ₹1.5 Cr

Note: This is a simplified estimate. Real retirement planning should account for inflation (typically 6% in India).

Retirement Planning Tips

  • Start Early: Starting at 25 vs. 35 can double your corpus due to compound interest.
  • Increase SIP with Income: Increase your SIP by 10% every year to account for salary hikes.
  • Diversify: PPF + NPS + Equity mutual funds provide safety, tax benefits, and growth.
  • Account for Inflation: Add 6% inflation buffer — ₹50,000 today equals ~₹2.4 lakh in 30 years.
  • Health Insurance: Get robust health coverage — medical costs are the biggest post-retirement risk.

The Power of Starting Early

If you start investing ₹5,000/month at age 25 (at 12% annual return), you will accumulate over ₹1.76 Crore by age 55. If you start the same at 35, you will only accumulate ₹54 Lakh — less than a third — in the same 20 remaining years. Time is your biggest asset in retirement planning.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. How much retirement corpus do I need?

A common rule: 25× your annual expenses using the 4% withdrawal rule. This calculator provides this estimate.

2. Should I factor in inflation?

Yes. Multiply your current corpus estimate by 2-4x based on the years to retirement to account for ~6% annual inflation.

3. When should I start saving for retirement?

Immediately. Starting at 25 vs. 35 makes a massive difference due to compounding. Even small monthly amounts accumulate significantly over decades.

4. What investments are best for retirement?

PPF and NPS for safety and tax benefits. Equity mutual funds (SIP) for long-term growth. A mix of all three is ideal.
