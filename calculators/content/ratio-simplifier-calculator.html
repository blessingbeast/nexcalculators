What is Ratio Simplification?

A ratio compares two quantities. Simplifying a ratio means dividing both values by their Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) to express the same relationship with the smallest possible whole numbers. For example, 24:36 simplifies to 2:3. Simplified ratios are easier to read, communicate, and use in further calculations.

Formula

GCD = Greatest Common Divisor of A and B

Simplified Ratio = (A ÷ GCD) : (B ÷ GCD)

Example Calculation

  • 48:72 → GCD = 24 → Simplified: 2:3
  • 100:250 → GCD = 50 → Simplified: 2:5
  • 15:45 → GCD = 15 → Simplified: 1:3

FAQs

What is the GCD method for simplifying ratios?

Find the largest number that divides both values without remainder (GCD), then divide both A and B by it. You can use the Euclidean algorithm to find GCD efficiently.

Are 3:4 and 6:8 equivalent ratios?

Yes! 6:8 simplifies to 3:4 (GCD=2). Equivalent ratios represent the same proportional relationship.
