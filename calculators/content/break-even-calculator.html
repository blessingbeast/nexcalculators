Every business owner needs to know their "magic number" — the point where costs are covered and profits begin. This Break-Even Calculator simplifies this complex analysis, helping you understand your risk and set realistic sales targets.

What is a Break-Even Point?

The break-even point is the production level where total revenues equal total expenses (fixed and variable). At this point, your business makes zero profit but incurs zero loss. Knowing this number is critical for launching new products, setting prices, and managing cash flow.

Formula

Break-Even Units = Fixed Costs / (Selling Price - Variable Cost per Unit)

Contribution Margin = Selling Price - Variable Cost

Example

Scenario: You run a coffee shop with ₹50,000 monthly rent (Fixed Cost). Each coffee sells for ₹200 (Price) and costs ₹80 to make (Variable Cost).

  • Fixed Costs = ₹50,000
  • Selling Price = ₹200
  • Variable Cost = ₹80
  • Contribution Margin = ₹200 - ₹80 = ₹120
  • Break-Even Units = ₹50,000 / ₹120 = 417 cups of coffee per month.

How to Use

  1. Fixed Costs: Monthly expenses that don't change (rent, salaries).
  2. Selling Price: How much you charge per unit.
  3. Variable Cost: The cost to produce one unit (materials, shipping).
  4. Click Calculate: Get your break-even point in units and currency.
