What is a Fuel Cost Per Trip Calculator?

A fuel cost per trip calculator estimates the total amount of money you'll spend on fuel for a specific journey. It combines three inputs — trip distance, your vehicle's mileage (fuel efficiency), and the current fuel price — to give you an accurate pre-trip budget figure.

Formula

Fuel Required = Trip Distance / Vehicle Mileage (km/l)

Total Fuel Cost = Fuel Required × Fuel Price per Litre

Example Calculation

Mumbai to Goa road trip: Distance = 590 km. Car mileage = 15 km/l. Petrol = ₹106/litre.

  • Fuel Required = 590/15 = 39.33 litres
  • Total Cost = 39.33 × ₹106 = ₹4,169 (one-way)
  • Round trip = ₹4,169 × 2 = ₹8,338

How to Use This Calculator

FAQs

How accurate is this fuel cost estimate?

It's highly accurate when you enter your real-world mileage (not manufacturer's claimed mileage). Real mileage is typically 10-20% lower than claimed due to traffic, AC use, and driving style.

Should I calculate one-way or round-trip?

For trips where you return by the same route, simply double the one-way fuel cost. Our calculator computes one-way by default — multiply by 2 for round trips.
