What is a Loan Affordability Calculator?

A loan affordability calculator determines the maximum loan amount you can take based on your current income, monthly expenses, the loan's interest rate, and tenure. Banks use the Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio (FOIR) — typically capping total EMIs at 40-50% of net monthly income — to decide loan eligibility. This calculator applies that same logic before you approach a lender.

Formula

Available EMI = (Monthly Income − Expenses) × 50%

Max Loan = EMI × [(1+r)^N − 1] / [r × (1+r)^N]

Where r = Monthly Interest Rate, N = Loan tenure in months

Example Calculation

Monthly Income: ₹80,000. Existing expenses: ₹30,000. Rate: 10% p.a. Tenure: 10 years.

  • Surplus = ₹80,000 − ₹30,000 = ₹50,000
  • Available EMI (50% FOIR) = ₹50,000 × 50% = ₹25,000
  • Max Loan ≈ ₹18,88,000 (at 10%, 120 months)

FAQs

What is FOIR and how does it affect loan approval?

FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) is the percentage of your income committed to EMIs. Most banks approve loans where total EMIs (including the new loan) don't exceed 40-50% of net monthly income.

Does my credit score affect loan affordability?

Indirectly, yes. A CIBIL score above 750 qualifies you for lower interest rates, which increases the affordable loan amount for the same EMI capacity.

How can I increase my loan eligibility?

Pay off existing EMIs, add a co-applicant with income (spouse/parent), improve your credit score, increase your down payment, and choose a longer tenure to lower the EMI.
