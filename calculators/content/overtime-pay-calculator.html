What is Overtime Pay?

Overtime pay is additional compensation for hours worked beyond the standard working day or week. Under India's Factories Act, overtime must be paid at twice (2x) the regular rate. Many private-sector and IT companies use 1.5x (time-and-a-half) under their employment contracts. Knowing how to calculate overtime accurately helps employees verify payslips and helps employers maintain compliant payroll.

Formula

Regular Pay = Regular Hours × Hourly Rate

Overtime Pay = Overtime Hours × Hourly Rate × Multiplier

Total Pay = Regular Pay + Overtime Pay

Example Calculation

Hourly Rate: ₹625/hr. Regular: 40 hours. Overtime: 10 hours at 1.5x.

  • Regular Pay = 40 × ₹625 = ₹25,000
  • Overtime Pay = 10 × ₹625 × 1.5 = ₹9,375
  • Total Weekly Pay = ₹34,375

FAQs

What is the overtime rate under India's Factories Act?

Under Section 59 of the Factories Act, 1948, workers must be paid at twice their ordinary rate for any hours worked beyond 9 hours per day or 48 hours per week. The minimum overtime rate in the organised sector is therefore 2x regular pay.

Can salaried employees claim overtime?

It depends on the employment contract. Most managerial and IT professionals have "at discretion" overtime clauses. Shop and Establishments Act-governed employees (retail, offices) may have specific overtime entitlements based on their state's rules.
