GST Calculator: Simplifying the One Nation, One Tax

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) was a watershed moment in India's economic history, aimed at removing cascading taxes and creating a unified market. Whether you are a business owner generating invoices, a freelancer quoting a client, or a consumer checking a restaurant bill, calculating GST accurately is part of daily life. This GST Calculator is a dual-purpose tool: it helps you add tax to a base price (Exclusive) and remove tax from a final price (Inclusive).

What is GST?

GST is an indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services. It is a comprehensive, multi-stage, destination-based tax:
Comprehensive: It subsumed almost all indirect taxes (Excise, VAT, Service Tax).
Multi-stage: It is imposed at every step in the production process where value is added.
Destination-based: The tax is collected by the state where the goods are consumed, not where they are made.

The 3 Components of GST

Understanding the split is crucial for businesses:

  • CGST (Central GST): Goes to the Central Government.
  • SGST (State GST): Goes to the State Government.
  • IGST (Integrated GST): Collected by Centre on Inter-state supply.

If you sell within the state (Intra-state): CGST + SGST applies (e.g., 9% + 9% = 18%).
If you sell to another state (Inter-state): IGST applies (flat 18%).

How to Calculate GST?

Method 1: GST Exclusive (Adding Tax)

Use this when you have a base price and need to generate an invoice.
Base Price: ₹1,000
GST Rate: 18%
Calculation: 1000 * (18/100) = ₹180 Tax.
Total Invoice Value: ₹1,180.

Method 2: GST Inclusive (Reverse Calculation)

Use this when you have a final MRP and want to know the tax component.
Total Price: ₹1,180
GST Rate: 18%
Formula: Total / (1 + Rate/100)
Calculation: 1180 / 1.18 = ₹1,000 (Base Price).
Tax Component: 1180 - 1000 = ₹180.

Why is Reverse Calculation Difficult?

Many people make the mistake of simply subtracting 18% from the total.
18% of 1180 is 212.4. If you subtract that, you get 967.6, which is WRONG.
Always use the reverse formula provided by our calculator to avoid accounting errors.

Current GST Slab Rates

  • 0% (Nil Rated): Fresh milk, vegetables, grains, salt.
  • 5%: Spices, oil, tea, coffee, life-saving drugs.
  • 12%: Processed food, computers, cycles.
  • 18%: Most services, soaps, toothpaste, electronics, capital goods. (Most common slab).
  • 28%: Luxury cars, ACs, cement, aerated drinks.

Input Tax Credit (ITC)

This is the soul of GST. It prevents "Tax on Tax".
If a manufacturer pays ₹100 tax on raw materials and collects ₹150 tax on finished goods, he only deposits ₹50 (150 - 100) to the government. This flow of credit ensures efficiency.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Who must register for GST?

Businesses with turnover > ₹40 Lakhs (Goods) or ₹20 Lakhs (Services). Also mandatory for e-commerce sellers.

2. What is the HSN Code?

Harmonized System of Nomenclature. A code to classify goods for taxation. Mandatory on invoices.

3. How many returns to file?

Regular businesses file GSTR-1 (Sales) and GSTR-3B (Summary) monthly. Small businesses (QRMP) can file quarterly.

4. Is GST applicable on exports?

Exports are "Zero Rated". You can export without paying tax (LUT) or pay and claim refund.

5. Does GST apply to old used cars?

Yes, margin scheme applies. GST is charged on the profit margin of the dealer, not the full value.

6. What is Composition Scheme?

For small businesses (turnover < 1.5 Cr). Pay flat generic rate (1% to 6%) on turnover but cannot allow ITC or collect tax from customers.

7. Can I correct a wrong invoice?

Yes, issue a Credit Note (to reduce value) or Debit Note (to increase value) linked to original invoice.

8. What is "Reverse Charge Mechanism" (RCM)?

Usually supplier pays tax. In RCM (e.g., legal services, transport), the recipient pays the tax directly to government.

9. Is petrol under GST?

No. Petroleum, Alcohol for human consumption, and Electricity are currently outside GST purview.

10. What is e-Way Bill?

A document required for movement of goods worth > ₹50,000. Generated from GST portal.
