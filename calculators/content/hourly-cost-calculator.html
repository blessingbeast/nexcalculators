What is Hourly Cost?

The true hourly cost of an employee includes far more than their salary. In India, employer PF (12%), gratuity provision (4.81%), office space, equipment, software licences, HR management, training, and management time all contribute to the real cost of a hire. Understanding this number helps businesses price their services to cover actual costs and make informed hiring vs. contracting decisions.

Formula

Total Annual Cost = Salary + Benefits + PF + Overhead

Hourly Cost = Total Annual Cost / Annual Working Hours

Example Calculation

Annual Salary: ₹6,00,000. Benefits/PF: ₹90,000. Overhead: ₹60,000. Hours: 2,080/year.

  • Total Annual Cost = ₹6,00,000 + ₹90,000 + ₹60,000 = ₹7,50,000
  • Hourly Cost = ₹7,50,000 / 2,080 = ₹361/hour

FAQs

What is the employer's PF contribution in India?

Employers contribute 12% of the employee's basic salary to EPF. Additionally, 4.81% goes towards EPS (Employee Pension Scheme). Total employer PF+EPS contribution is approximately 13-14% of basic salary.

Why is true hourly cost 1.5-2x the salary-based rate?

When you add employer PF (13%), gratuity (4.81%), office space (10-15%), equipment (5%), software (5%), HR/management overhead (5-10%), and training (3-5%), the total true cost often runs 40-55% above the gross salary.
