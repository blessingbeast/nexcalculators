What Is Savings Interest?

Savings interest is the return a bank or financial institution pays you for storing your money with them. Instead of letting your cash sit idle in a zero-interest current account, a savings account, FD, or RD pays you a percentage of your deposited money as a reward over time. This is one of the safest and most reliable ways to grow wealth, especially in India where bank deposits are insured by DICGC up to ₹5 Lakhs.

Using a savings interest calculator online free helps you compare bank interest rates and project exactly how much your deposit will be worth at maturity — so you can make the right choice before locking in your funds.

The Compound Interest Formula

Most banks compound interest on a quarterly basis in India. Our tool applies the following formula to give you an accurate maturity projection:

Maturity Amount (A) = P × (1 + r/n)^(n×t)

P = Principal, r = Annual Rate (decimal), n = Compounding Periods per Year, t = Time in Years

Total Interest Earned = A - P

Step-by-Step FD Example

Let's use the savings interest calculator online free to project a Fixed Deposit at SBI. You deposit ₹2,00,000 for 3 years at 6.8% per annum, compounded quarterly.

  • P = ₹2,00,000, r = 0.068, n = 4 (quarterly), t = 3.
  • Quarterly Rate = 6.8% / 4 = 1.7%.
  • Total compounding periods = 4 × 3 = 12.
  • A = ₹2,00,000 × (1 + 0.017)^12 = ₹2,00,000 × 1.2238 = ₹2,44,760 (approx.)
  • Total Interest Earned = ₹44,760 over 3 years.

Simple vs Compound Savings: Why It Matters

For a ₹1,00,000 deposit at 7% for 10 years:

  • Simple Interest: ₹1,00,000 + ₹70,000 = ₹1,70,000.
  • Compound (Annual): ₹1,96,715 — that's ₹26,715 more from compounding alone.
  • Compound (Quarterly): ₹2,00,160 — even more, because interest is added 4× a year.

This is why compounding frequency matters enormously and why our savings interest calculator online free lets you select the exact compounding mode.

FAQs

1. Is FD interest taxable in India?

Yes. Interest earned on FDs is added to your "Income from Other Sources" and taxed at your applicable income tax slab rate. Banks also deduct 10% TDS if annual interest exceeds ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens).

2. What is an RD (Recurring Deposit)?

An RD allows you to deposit a fixed smaller amount every month instead of a lump sum. For an RD, the total interest is slightly lower since early installments compound longer than later ones. Our savings calculator supports RD estimation.

3. Which Indian bank offers the highest FD rate?

Small Finance Banks (like Jana, AU, or Unity SFB) often offer 8-9% FD rates, while large nationalized banks (SBI, BOB) offer 6-7%. Always check the bank's current FD rate chart as rates change frequently.

4. Is my FD safe if the bank fails?

Under DICGC (Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation), your deposits are insured up to ₹5 Lakhs per bank per depositor. Amounts above this are at risk if the bank goes under.
