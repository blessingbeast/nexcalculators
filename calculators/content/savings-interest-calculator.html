What is Savings Interest?

Savings interest is the compensation a financial institution pays you for the privilege of using your money. Unlike a regular checking account, a savings account usually pays a higher rate, often compounding monthly or annually. Understanding whether your interest is "Simple" or "Compound" is key to maximizing your wealth.

Formula

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

A = Final Balance, P = Principal, r = Annual Interest Rate, n = Compounding Periods, t = Time in Years.

Example

Scenario: You deposit ₹50,000 at a 4% interest rate compounded monthly for 5 years.

  • Initial Deposit = ₹50,000
  • Annual Rate = 4% (0.04)
  • Compounding = Monthly (12 times per year)
  • Duration = 5 Years
  • Final Balance = ₹61,049.83

FAQs

1. What is this calculator?

A tool for personal finance management to estimate the growth of a bank savings account.

2. How does it work?

It uses the compound interest formula to factor in the "interest on interest" growth over time.

3. What is compounding?

Compounding is the frequency at which the earned interest is added back to your balance to earn more interest in the next period.
