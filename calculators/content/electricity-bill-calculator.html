Electricity Bill Calculator: Shock-Proof Your Finances

We rely on electricity for everything, but the monthly bill often remains a mystery. Why is the bill higher in summer? What happens if you run the AC for 8 hours instead of 4? This Electricity Bill Calculator helps you estimate your monthly power costs based on your consumption (Units) and local tariff rates.

Understanding "Units" (kWh)

The electric meter measures in "Units".
1 Unit = 1 Kilowatt-Hour (kWh).
This means using a 1000 Watt (1 kW) appliance for 1 Hour consumes 1 Unit.

Examples:
- 2000W AC running for 1 hour = 2 Units.
- 10W LED Bulb running for 100 hours = 1 Unit.

Tariff Slabs

Electricity is charged in slabs in most places. The more you use, the higher the rate.
Example:
0-100 units: ₹3/unit.
101-200 units: ₹5/unit.
>200 units: ₹8/unit.
This "Telescopic Pricing" encourages conservation.

Fixed Charges

Apart from energy charges, bills include "Fixed Charges" based on your sanctioned load (e.g., ₹100 per kW per month) and various taxes (Electricity Duty).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. How to reduce bill?

Switch to 5-star appliances, use LED bulbs, and keep AC at 24°C.

2. What uses most power?

Heating/Cooling appliances: AC, Geyser, Heater, Iron.

3. Inverter AC saving?

Yes, Inverter ACs save 20-30% by running the compressor at variable speeds.

4. Vampire power?

Appliances in "Standby" (TV, Microwave) still consume small power.

5. Sanctioned Load?

The max power you are allowed to draw. Exceeding it attracts penalties.

6. Solar benefits?

Rooftop solar can bring your bill to near zero via "Net Metering".

7. Smart Meter?

A digital meter that sends readings automatically to the provider.

8. Peak hours?

Some industrial connections have higher rates during peak evening hours.

9. Fan regulator?

Electronic regulators save power. Old resistive ones wasted power as heat.

10. Star rating?

BEE Star Rating (1-5). 5 Star is most efficient.
