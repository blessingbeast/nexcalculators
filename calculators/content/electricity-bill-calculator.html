What Is an Electricity Bill Calculator?

An electricity bill calculator estimates your monthly electricity charges based on the number of units (kWh) consumed and the rate per unit set by your electricity distribution company (DISCOM). This helps you plan your monthly budget, identify high-usage appliances, and track savings from energy-efficient habits.

Electricity Bill Formula

Electricity Bill = Units Consumed × Rate per Unit

Where: 1 Unit = 1 kWh (using a 1000W appliance for 1 hour)

Note: Your actual bill may include additional charges like meter rent, fixed charges, and applicable taxes — add these on top of the base calculation.

Example Calculation

Scenario: You consumed 250 units this month. Your state rate is ₹6.50 per unit.

  • Base Bill = 250 × ₹6.50 = ₹1,625
  • Meter rent + fixed charges = ~₹100 (approximate)
  • Estimated Total Bill = ₹1,725

How to Reduce Your Electricity Bill

  • Switch to LED: LED bulbs use 80% less energy than incandescent bulbs.
  • Use 5-Star Appliances: BEE 5-star rated appliances consume significantly less power.
  • Turn Off Standby: TVs, chargers on standby still consume 5-10% of normal power.
  • Natural Cooling: Fans over ACs for mild weather — huge savings on peak summer bills.
  • Solar Panels: A 3kW solar system can eliminate 80-100% of monthly bills in sunny regions.

Understanding Electricity Slabs in India

Most Indian states use a slab-based tariff. The first 100 units may cost ₹3/unit, the next 200 at ₹5/unit, and above 300 at ₹7/unit. This means your effective rate increases as consumption rises — encouraging conservation.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is 1 unit of electricity?

1 unit = 1 kWh. Using a 1000W appliance (like a hair dryer) for exactly 1 hour consumes 1 unit.

2. How do I find my rate per unit?

Check your electricity bill or visit your state DISCOM's website for current tariff slabs.

3. Does this include taxes?

No. This gives a base estimate. Add applicable duty, meter rent (~₹50-150/month), and state taxes for your exact bill.

4. Is this electricity calculator free?

Yes, completely free. No login required, no data stored.

5. How accurate is this calculator?

Very accurate for base calculation. Actual bills vary due to slab rates and additional charges.
