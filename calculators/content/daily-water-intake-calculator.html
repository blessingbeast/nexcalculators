What is Daily Water Intake?

Daily water intake is the total volume of water your body needs to maintain optimal hydration, regulate temperature, support organ function, and remove waste. The right amount varies significantly based on body weight, activity level, climate, and health conditions — one-size-fits-all "8 glasses a day" advice is overly simplistic.

Formula

Base Water Need (litres) = Body Weight (kg) × 0.033

Add 0.35-0.5 L for each 30 minutes of exercise | Add 0.5 L in hot/humid climates

Example Calculation

  • Person: 68 kg, moderately active (30 min exercise/day).
  • Base = 68 × 0.033 = 2.24 L
  • Exercise bonus = +0.4 L
  • Total Daily Target = 2.64 L (≈ 11 glasses of 240 ml)

Real Life Use Cases

  • Indian Summer Hydration: In May-June heat, daily needs can exceed 3-4 litres due to excessive sweating.
  • Athletes: Runners and cyclists need to pre-hydrate, hydrate during, and rehydrate post-workout.
  • Weight Management: Drinking water before meals can reduce appetite and support weight loss.
  • Kidney Health: Adequate hydration prevents kidney stones, especially in India's heat.

FAQs

Does tea, coffee, and juice count as water?

Water-based beverages partially count, but caffeinated drinks (tea, coffee) have mild diuretic effects. Fruits and vegetables also contribute water. Pure water remains the most effective hydration source.

What are the signs of dehydration?

Dark yellow urine, headaches, fatigue, dry mouth, dizziness, reduced concentration, and skin that doesn't bounce back when pinched are all signs of dehydration.
