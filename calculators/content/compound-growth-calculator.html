Einstein called compounding the "eighth wonder of the world," and for good reason. This Compound Growth Calculator helps you visualize how an initial sum grows exponentially when the growth from each period is reinvested to generate more growth.

What is Compound Growth?

Compound growth is the process where the value of an asset increases over time because the returns (interest, capital gains, or dividends) are added back to the original balance to earn even more returns in the next period. Unlike simple growth, which is linear, compound growth is exponential, meaning it "accelerates" the longer you leave it untouched.

Formula

Final Value = Initial Value × (1 + Growth Rate / 100) ^ Years

Note: "^" denotes "to the power of"

Example

Scenario: You invest ₹1,00,000 in an asset that grows at 10% annually for 10 years.

  • Initial Value = ₹1,00,000
  • Growth Rate = 10%
  • Years = 10
  • Calculation: ₹1,00,000 × (1.10)^10 = ₹1,00,000 × 2.5937
  • Final Value = ₹2,59,374

How to Use
