Unit Converter: The Universal Translator for Math

Science, engineering, and trade rely on standards. But the world is split. The US uses Imperial (Miles, Pounds, Gallons) while most of the world uses Metric (Kilometers, Kilograms, Liters). This Unit Converter bridges the gap, allowing you to instantly translate values between these systems with high precision.

The Metric System (SI)

Based on powers of 10. It is logical and universal.
1000m = 1km. 100cm = 1m.

The Imperial System

Based on historical references (e.g., a "foot" was the length of a king's foot). Non-decimal.
12 inches = 1 foot. 3 feet = 1 yard. 1760 yards = 1 mile.

Common Conversions

  • Distance: 1 Mile ≈ 1.61 Kilometers.
  • Weight: 1 Kilogram ≈ 2.204 Pounds (lbs).
  • Volume: 1 Gallon (US) ≈ 3.785 Liters.
  • Temperature: °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32.

Why Conversion Matters

In 1999, NASA lost the $125 million Mars Climate Orbiter because one team used Metric units and another used Imperial units for force calculation. Precision matters.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Why does US use Imperial?

Historical reasons and cost of industrial retooling.

2. Celsius vs Centigrade?

Same thing. Celsius is the modern name.

3. Metric Cup vs US Cup?

Metric Cup = 250ml. US Cup ≈ 236ml.

4. Stone?

British unit for weight. 1 Stone = 14 lbs = 6.35 kg.

5. Kelvin?

Scientific absolute temperature. 0K is absolute zero. K = C + 273.15.

6. Nautical Mile?

Based on earth's circumference. 1 NM = 1.852 km.

7. Acre?

Unit of land. 1 Acre ≈ 4047 m².

8. Carat?

Unit for gems. 1 Carat = 0.2 grams.

9. Horsepower?

1 HP (Imperial) ≈ 746 Watts.

10. Gigabyte (GB)?

In decimal (storage), 10^9 bytes. In binary (RAM), 2^30 bytes.
