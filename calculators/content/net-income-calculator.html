What is Net Income?

Net income (also called take-home pay or after-tax income) is what you retain after the government and your employer deduct income tax, provident fund contributions, professional tax, and other statutory deductions. It's the foundation of all personal financial planning — every budget, savings rate, and EMI is measured against this figure.

Formula

Net Annual Income = Gross Annual Income − Employee PF − Income Tax − Other Deductions

Example Calculation

  • Gross Annual: ₹9,00,000. PF: ₹64,800 (12% of basic ₹45,000). Income Tax: ₹62,500. Others: ₹12,000.
  • Net Annual = ₹9,00,000 − ₹64,800 − ₹62,500 − ₹12,000 = ₹7,60,700
  • Monthly: ₹7,60,700 / 12 = ₹63,392/month

FAQs

How can I increase my net income in India?

Maximise all deductions: Section 80C (₹1.5L), NPS (₹50K under 80CCD), health insurance (₹25K under 80D), and HRA claims. Switching to the old tax regime is beneficial if your deductions exceed ₹3L.

Is PF a deduction or a savings?

Both. Employee PF reduces your take-home but is saved in your EPF account — it's your own money that earns 8.25% interest annually. It's a forced long-term saving that also provides tax benefits.
