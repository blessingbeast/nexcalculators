What is a Loan Interest Calculator?

A loan interest calculator is a financial tool that computes the total interest you will pay over the entire life of a loan. When banks and NBFCs advertise low interest rates, the actual interest paid — especially on long-tenure loans — can be significantly more than the principal itself. This tool makes that hidden cost visible instantly.

There are two primary methods of calculating loan interest: Simple Interest (used for short-term loans and informal lending) and Reducing Balance (used by virtually all banks for personal, home, and car loans).

Formula

Simple Interest: SI = (P × R × T) / 100

Total Interest (EMI-based loan): = (EMI × N) − Principal

Where EMI = [P × r × (1+r)^N] / [(1+r)^N − 1], r = monthly rate, N = months

Example Calculation

You take a ₹5,00,000 personal loan at 12% per annum for 3 years.

  • Monthly Rate (r) = 12% / 12 = 1% = 0.01
  • Months (N) = 3 × 12 = 36
  • EMI = [5,00,000 × 0.01 × (1.01)^36] / [(1.01)^36 − 1] = ₹16,607
  • Total Paid = ₹16,607 × 36 = ₹5,97,852
  • Total Interest = ₹5,97,852 − ₹5,00,000 = ₹97,852

FAQs

What is the difference between flat rate and reducing balance interest?

Flat rate charges interest on the full principal throughout the tenure. Reducing balance charges interest only on the outstanding principal — so interest decreases as you repay. A 7% flat rate is roughly equivalent to a 13% reducing balance rate.

Does a longer tenure save me money?

A longer tenure reduces your EMI but massively increases total interest paid. A ₹20L home loan at 8.5% for 20 years pays ~₹22L in interest. The same loan for 10 years pays ~₹10L — saving ₹12L.

Can I reduce my loan interest?

Yes — maintain a good CIBIL score (750+), make part-prepayments when possible, negotiate rate reductions after 2-3 years of good repayment, and consider balance transfers to lower-rate lenders.
