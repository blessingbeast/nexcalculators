What is Overtime Pay?

Overtime pay is the additional compensation paid to employees for hours worked beyond their contractually specified standard schedule. In India, most private and public sectors follow a 48-hour maximum work week (8 hours per day, 6 days a week). Any time worked beyond this limit is legally mandated to be paid at a "Premium Rate," typically **Double the Ordinary Rate** (2x) as per the Factories Act, though many corporate contracts use 1.5x (Time-and-a-Half).

Using an overtime calculator india salary tool helps you verify your monthly paycheck and ensure you are being paid exactly what you've earned for your extra effort.

The Overtime Formula

Calculating your premium earnings is a simple multiplication of three factors. Our online tool automates this to help you plan your monthly budget:

Overtime Pay = Hourly Rate × Overtime Hours × Multiplier

Total Earnings = Regular Salary + Overtime Pay

Overtime Laws in India: What You Should Know

According to the Factories Act of 1948 and various Shops and Establishments Acts across Indian states (like Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Delhi), workers are entitled to twice their ordinary rate for every hour of overtime. While some "managerial" roles might be exempt in certain contracts, most operational and technical staff are entitled to this benefit. Always check your offer letter to identify your specific multiplier.

FAQs

1. What is the standard overtime multiplier in India?

Legally, under the Factories Act, it is 2.0 (Double Pay). However, many private IT and service companies use 1.5 as per their internal HR policies. Our calculator lets you enter either.

2. Does overtime pay include HRA or other allowances?

In most cases, 'Ordinary Rate of Wages' for overtime includes Basic Salary + Dearness Allowance (DA), but usually excludes HRA, travel allowance, and bonus.

3. How do I find my 'Hourly Rate' from a monthly salary?

Divide your monthly Basic + DA by 26 (working days) and then by 8 (hours per day). Or, use our Salary to Hourly Calculator available on this site.

4. Is overtime pay taxable?

Yes. Overtime pay is added to your 'Income from Salary' and is taxed at your applicable income tax slab rate for the financial year.
