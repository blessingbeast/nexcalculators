What is a Weighted Score?

A weighted score assigns different levels of importance (weights) to different components before averaging them. Unlike a simple average (where all items count equally), a weighted average reflects the relative significance of each element. University grading systems, performance reviews, financial indices, and quality scoring all use weighted calculations.

Formula

Weighted Score = Σ(Score × Weight) / Σ(Weights)

Example Calculation

A student scores 80 in midterm (40% weight) and 70 in final exam (60% weight).

  • Weighted Score = (80×40 + 70×60) / (40+60) = (3200 + 4200) / 100 = 74
  • Note: A simple average would give 75 — but the final exam matters more here.

FAQs

Do weights need to add up to 100?

Ideally yes. If they don't, the formula still works by dividing by the sum of weights — but percentages are the clearest way to define importance.

When should I use weighted average instead of simple average?

Use weighted average when components have unequal importance — like a final exam counting more than a quiz, or a high-value investment dominating a portfolio.
