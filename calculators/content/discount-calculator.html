What Is a Discount Calculator?

A discount calculator is a tool that computes the final price of an item after a percentage or fixed amount is deducted from its original price. It eliminates mental math errors and helps you compare deals quickly — whether you're shopping online or in a store.

Discount Formula Used

The discount calculation uses two simple formulas:

Savings = Original Price × (Discount % ÷ 100)

Final Price = Original Price − Savings

Discount % = (Discount Amount ÷ Original Price) × 100

Example Calculation

Scenario: A shirt is priced at ₹1,999. It has a 30% discount tag.

  • Savings = ₹1,999 × 30 ÷ 100 = ₹599.70
  • Final Price = ₹1,999 − ₹599.70 = ₹1,399.30

So you save ₹599.70 — the calculator shows this instantly.

Types of Discounts

1. Percentage Discount

The most common type. "20% Off" means you pay 80% of the original price. Example: ₹100 item with 20% off → you pay ₹80.

2. Stacked/Double Discount

Stores often say "50% + 20% Off". This does NOT mean 70% off. First 50% off gives ₹50. Then 20% off that ₹50 = ₹10 more off. Total paid: ₹40. Total discount: 60%.

3. Buy X Get Y Free (BOGO)

"Buy 1 Get 1 Free" = 50% Off overall. "Buy 2 Get 1 Free" = 33.33% Off. Formula: (Free Items / Total Items) × 100.

The Psychology of Pricing

Retailers use specific tricks to make discounts look bigger:
– Anchoring: Show a high "Original Price" (crossed out) to make the discounted price look like a steal.
– Charm Pricing: Using ₹99 instead of ₹100 — it feels significantly cheaper.
– Artificial Urgency: "Offer ends tonight!" to pressure quick decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. How do I calculate percentage off?

(Difference ÷ Original Price) × 100. Example: ₹100 item now at ₹80 → (20/100) × 100 = 20% off.

2. Is 50% + 50% = Free?

No! It equals 75% off. (50% of 100 = 50. 50% of remaining 50 = 25. Total paid = 25).

3. Discount vs Cash Back — which is better?

Discount reduces what you pay now. Cashback returns money later. Instant discount is better for cash flow.

4. Is Buy 1 Get 1 better than 50% off?

Mathematically equal, but BOGO forces you to take 2 items — only better if you need both.

5. Is GST calculated on the discounted price?

Yes, as per Indian GST rules, tax is generally calculated on the transaction value (discounted price).
