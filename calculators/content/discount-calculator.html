The Multi-Step Discount Formula

To find the final "landed cost" of an item, you need to follow a specific order of operations. Our tool handles these layers of math for you:

Step 1: Discounted Price = Original Price - (Original Price × Discount %)

Step 2: Final Price (with GST) = Discounted Price + (Discounted Price × GST %)

Step-by-Step Example: Festival Shopping

Let's look at a common shopping scenario using the discount calculator with gst included. You see an electronic gadget priced at ₹10,000 with a 20% discount, and you know there is an 18% GST rate.

  • Step 1: Calculate the savings. ₹10,000 × 0.20 = ₹2,000 off.
  • Step 2: Find the discounted price. ₹10,000 - ₹2,000 = ₹8,000.
  • Step 3: Calculate the GST on ₹8,000. ₹8,000 × 0.18 = ₹1,440.
  • Step 4: Find the total amount payable. ₹8,000 + ₹1,440 = ₹9,440.

While the "20% Off" made it look like you'd only pay ₹8,000, the addition of GST means your actual out-of-pocket expense is ₹9,440.

Common Traps: "Discount on Discount"

One frequent marketing trick is offering "50% + 20% Off". Many shoppers think this means 70% off. However, it is actually 60% off. The first 50% discount reduces a ₹1,000 item to ₹500. The second 20% discount is applied to that ₹500, taking off another ₹100. The final price is ₹400. Using our discount calculator with gst included ensures you never fall for misleading math.

FAQs

1. Is GST calculated before or after the discount?

In most tax jurisdictions, including India, GST is calculated on the 'Transaction Value' (the price actually paid after the discount is applied).

2. What is "Incl. of all taxes"?

If an item says "Inclusive of all taxes," then the GST is already built into the MRP. Our calculator can help you find the 'base price' by working backwards.

3. How do I calculate a fixed-amount discount?

If you have a coupon for ₹500 off, you can find the percentage by dividing 500 by the original price. Or, simply use our basic discount tool for fixed deductions.
