Discount Calculator: Never Miss a Deal

In a world of "Flash Sales", "Black Friday", and "End of Season" offers, calculating the final price of an item can get confusing. Is "Buy 2 Get 1 Free" better than "Flat 40% Off"? What is the final price of a ₹1999 shirt with a 15% discount? This Discount Calculator is your shopping companion, ensuring you know exactly what you pay and how much you save.

How Discounts Work

A discount is simply a reduction in the original price (List Price or MRP). It is a marketing tool used to increase sales volume.
Formula: Savings = Original Price × (Discount % / 100).
Final Price: Original Price - Savings.

Types of Discounts

1. Percentage Discount

The most common type. "20% Off".
Example: Price ₹100, Discount 20%. You pay ₹80.

2. Double Discount (Stacking)

Stores often say "50% + 20% Off". This does NOT mean 70% Off.
It means: First take 50% off (Price becomes 50). Then take 20% off the *remaining* 50 (which is 10).
Total You Pay: ₹40. Total Discount: 60%.

3. Buy X Get Y Free (BOGO)

"Buy 1 Get 1 Free" = 50% Off.
"Buy 2 Get 1 Free" = 33.33% Off.
Formula: (Free Items / Total Items) × 100.

The Psychology of Pricing

Retailers use specific tricks to make discounts look bigger.
- Anchoring: Showing a high "Original Price" (crossed out) to make the discounted price look like a steal.
- Charm Pricing: Using ₹99 instead of ₹100.

Conclusion

Don't let the red "Sale" tags fool you. Use this calculator to check the real math behind the offer. A deal is only good if the final price matches the value you get.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. How to calculate % off?

(Difference / Original Price) × 100.

2. Is 50% + 50% = Free?

No! It is 75% off. (50% of 100 is 50. 50% of remaining 50 is 25. Total paid 25).

3. Discount vs Cash Back?

Discount reduces what you pay now. Cashback returns money later. Discount is better for cashflow.

4. Is Buy 1 Get 1 better than 50% off?

Mathematically equal, but BOGO forces you to take 2 items.

5. Tax on discounted items?

GST is usually calculated on the discounted price (Transaction Value).

6. Rebate vs Discount?

Rebate is usually post-purchase (mail-in). Discount is instant.

7. Negative discount?

That's a surcharge or "Premium pricing".

8. Wholesale discount?

Cheaper price for buying in bulk.

9. Early bird?

Discount for paying/booking early.

10. How to calculate manually?

Move decimal left 1 spot for 10%. Multiply to get 20%, 30%.
