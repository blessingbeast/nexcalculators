What Is a Date Difference Calculator?

A date difference calculator computes the exact number of calendar days between two dates. It accounts for all months (including shorter ones like February), leap years, and varying month lengths — giving you a precise answer that manual calculation can easily get wrong.

Date Difference Formula

Days Between Dates = End Date − Start Date

Weeks = Days ÷ 7

Months ≈ Days ÷ 30.44 (average days per month)

Example Calculation

Scenario: How many days from January 1, 2026 to March 20, 2026?

  • January: 31 days (full month)
  • February: 28 days (2026 is not a leap year)
  • March: 20 days (partial)
  • Total = 31 + 28 + 20 = 79 days (or ~11.3 weeks, ~2.6 months)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Does this calculator account for leap years?

Yes. February 29 is automatically included when the year is a leap year (divisible by 4, except centuries not divisible by 400).

2. Are both start and end dates included?

The result shows days between the dates. To include both endpoints (like a rental period), add 1 to the result.

3. Can I calculate future dates?

Yes. You can calculate the difference between any two past, present, or future dates.

4. How do I find business days (excluding weekends)?

Divide total days by 7, multiply by 5 (weekdays), and add remaining weekdays. For accuracy, use a dedicated business day calculator.

Yes, completely free. No login required, no data stored.
