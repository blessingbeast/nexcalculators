Savings Goal Calculator: Dream it, Plan it

We all have goals. A dream wedding, a world tour, a new car, or a down payment for a house. But a goal without a plan is just a wish. This Savings Goal Calculator works backward: You tell it how much you need and when, and it tells you how much to save every month to get there.

The Math of Discipline

Saving is not about what is left after spending. It is about spending what is left after saving.
Formula: Monthly Savings = (Target Amount - Current Savings) / Months Remaining.

Where to Park Short-Term Savings?

  • < 1 Year: Savings Account or Liquid Funds. Safety is priority.
  • 1 - 3 Years: Recurring Deposit (RD) or Short Term Debt Funds.
  • > 5 Years: Equity Mutual Funds (SIP).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. SMART Goals?

Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound.

2. Inflation factor?

Calculator assumes today's cost. Add 6%/year buffer for future cost.

3. Sinking Fund?

Saving small amounts monthly for a known future expense (like insurance premium).

4. Emergency Fund?

3-6 months of expenses covering job loss. First goal for everyone.

5. Pay yourself first?

Automate savings to move out of salary account on Day 1.

6. High-Yield Savings?

Some banks offer 7% on savings. Good for goals.

7. Tax impact?

Remember that RD/FD interest is taxable, reducing effective accumulation.

8. 52 Week Challenge?

Fun way to save. Save ₹10 week 1, ₹20 week 2... ₹520 week 52.

9. Borrowing for goals?

Avoid taking loans for "Wants" (vacation/wedding).

10. Tracking?

Review progress every quarter.
