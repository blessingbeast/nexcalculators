What Is a Ratio?

A ratio is a comparison of two quantities, showing how many times one value contains or is contained by another. Written as A:B (read "A to B"), it expresses the relative size of two values without specifying their actual amounts. For example, a ratio of 3:1 means the first value is three times the second.

How to Simplify a Ratio

Simplified Ratio = A ÷ GCD : B ÷ GCD

Where GCD = Greatest Common Divisor of A and B

Example: 12:8 → GCD(12,8) = 4 → Simplified = 3:2

Example Calculation

Scenario: Simplify the ratio 24:36.

  • Find GCD of 24 and 36 = 12
  • 24 ÷ 12 = 2
  • 36 ÷ 12 = 3
  • Simplified Ratio = 2:3

Real-Life Uses of Ratios

  • Recipes: Scaling up or down — 2:1 ratio of flour to butter.
  • Finance: Debt-to-equity ratio, price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio, current ratio.
  • Maps: Scale ratios like 1:50,000 (1 cm = 500 m in reality).
  • Screens: Aspect ratios like 16:9 (widescreen) or 4:3 (old TV format).
  • Construction: Cement to sand ratios in concrete mixing (1:2:4).
  • Sports: Win-loss ratios and performance metrics.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is GCD and why is it used for ratios?

GCD (Greatest Common Divisor) is the largest number that divides both values evenly. Dividing both by GCD gives the simplest form.

2. Is ratio the same as fraction?

Similar concept, different use. Fraction (3/4) shows part of a whole. Ratio (3:4) compares two separate quantities.

3. What is a 1:1 ratio?

Equal amounts. For example, 1:1 cement to water means equal volumes of both.

4. How to find equivalent ratios?

Multiply or divide both sides by the same number. E.g., 2:3 = 4:6 = 6:9 = 10:15.
