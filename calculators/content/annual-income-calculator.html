What is Annual Income?

Annual income is the total money you earn from all sources in one financial year (April to March in India). For income tax purposes, this includes salary (under the head "Salaries"), freelance/business income ("Business & Profession"), rental income ("House Property"), interest (Other Sources"), and capital gains from investments. Knowing your total annual income is essential for ITR filing and financial planning.

Formula

Annual Income = (Monthly Salary × 12) + Annual Bonus + Other Annual Income

Example Calculation

  • Monthly Salary: ₹55,000 × 12 = ₹6,60,000
  • Annual Bonus: ₹1,20,000
  • Freelance Income: ₹2,40,000
  • Rental Income: ₹1,44,000
  • Total Annual Income = ₹11,64,000

FAQs

What income do I need to file an ITR in India?

You must file an ITR if your gross total income exceeds ₹2.5 Lakhs (₹3L for 60+ years, ₹5L for 80+ years). Even below this threshold, filing is recommended for loan and visa applications.

Is annual income the same as taxable income?

No. Taxable income = Annual income − Deductions (80C, HRA, NPS, etc.). These instruments reduce your taxable base before calculating the tax owed.
