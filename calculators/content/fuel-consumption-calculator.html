What is Fuel Consumption?

Fuel consumption is the amount of fuel a vehicle uses over a given distance, typically expressed as litres per 100 km (L/100km) or kilometres per litre (km/l). Knowing your actual fuel consumption helps you budget travel costs accurately, identify when your vehicle needs servicing (drop in mileage = warning sign), and compare vehicles before buying.

Formula

Fuel Efficiency (km/l) = Distance (km) / Fuel Used (litres)

Fuel Consumption (L/100km) = (Fuel Used / Distance) × 100

Example Calculation

  • Distance: 450 km. Fuel used: 30 litres.
  • Efficiency = 450/30 = 15 km/l
  • L/100km = (30/450) × 100 = 6.67 L/100km

FAQs

What is a good fuel efficiency for an Indian car?

Economy hatchbacks: 18-25 km/l. Mid-range sedans: 15-20 km/l. SUVs: 12-17 km/l. Bikes and scooters: 40-60 km/l.

How can I improve my vehicle's fuel efficiency?

Maintain optimal tyre pressure, drive at 60-80 km/h on highways, avoid aggressive acceleration/braking, service the engine regularly, and reduce AC use in city traffic.
