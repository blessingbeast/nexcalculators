What is Body Mass Index (BMI)?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a screening tool that estimates body fat based on weight relative to height. It's calculated by dividing a person's weight (in kg) by the square of their height (in metres). BMI is used by doctors, nutritionists, insurance companies, and fitness experts as a quick initial assessment of healthy weight status.

Formula

BMI = Weight (kg) / Height² (m²)

Example: 70 kg, 170 cm → BMI = 70 / (1.70)² = 70 / 2.89 = 24.2

BMI Categories (WHO)

  • Underweight: BMI below 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 – 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25.0 – 29.9
  • Obese (Class I): BMI 30.0 – 34.9
  • Obese (Class II+): BMI 35.0 and above

FAQs

Is BMI accurate for all body types?

BMI is a useful screening tool but doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat. Athletes and bodybuilders often have high BMI despite very low body fat. It should be used alongside other measurements like waist circumference and body fat percentage.

Should Indians use different BMI cutoffs?

Yes. Research suggests Asian populations (including Indians) face metabolic risks at lower BMI values. Many Indian health guidelines recommend the "overweight" cutoff at BMI 23 and "obese" at 25 for Indians.
