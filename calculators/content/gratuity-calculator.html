Gratuity Calculator: Reward for Loyalty

Gratuity is a monetary benefit given by an employer to an employee for complying with the concept of long-term service. It is governed by the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972. It is a lump sum amount paid when you leave a job after serving for at least 5 years.

Eligibility

1. Must be eligible for superannuation/retirement/resignation.
2. Must have completed 5 continuous years of service.
(Note: In case of death/disablement, the 5-year rule is waived).

Formula

Gratuity = (15 × Last Drawn Salary × Tenure) / 26.
- 15: Wages for 15 days.
- 26: Working days in a month.
- Salary: Basic Pay + Dearness Allowance (DA).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is it taxable?

Tax-free up to ₹20 Lakhs for private employees. Fully tax-free for Govt employees.

2. 4 years 10 months?

Usually rounded off. Some courts say 240 days in 5th year = 1 year. But safer to complete 5 full years.

3. Is it mandatory?

Yes, for any organization with >10 employees.

4. CTC includes Gratuity?

Yes, usually part of CTC shown in offer letter, but paid only on exit.

5. Notice period counts?

Yes, date of relieving is considered.

6. 6 months rule?

In tenure calculation, >6 months is rounded to 1 year.

7. Nominee?

Required (Form F) so family gets it in case of death.

8. Forfeiture?

Can be forfeited if terminated for "riotous or disorderly conduct".

9. Contractor?

Contract employees are also eligible.

10. Capping?

Max statutory limit is ₹20 Lakhs. Employers can pay more (taxable).
